What to Pack for a Safari in Uganda

Prepare confidently for gorilla trekking, game drives, lakes, and unforgettable adventures

By Explore Uganda Safari Desk·Published March 1, 2026

A safari in Uganda is not just a trip—it is a journey through one of Africa’s most diverse and breathtaking countries. From the misty forests of Bwindi to the open savannahs of Murchison Falls, and the calm waters of Lake Bunyonyi, every destination offers a completely different experience.

Because of this diversity, packing for Uganda requires more thought than a typical holiday. In a single week, you may find yourself trekking through dense rainforest, cruising along the Nile, exploring national parks, and relaxing by scenic lakes. Each environment comes with its own demands.

Many travelers underestimate this—and it affects their experience. Being too hot, getting bitten by insects, or lacking the right footwear can quickly turn excitement into discomfort. On the other hand, packing correctly allows you to move freely, stay comfortable, and fully enjoy every moment.

This guide is designed to ensure that you are completely prepared—whether you are traveling from abroad or exploring Uganda as a local tourist rediscovering your country.

Clothing for Uganda: Adapting to Changing Landscapes

Uganda’s climate is generally warm, but it is far from uniform. Early mornings in places like Murchison Falls can be cool, midday temperatures in the savannah can be intense, and forest regions like Bwindi are often humid and damp. Your clothing must be versatile enough to handle all of these conditions.

Neutral-colored clothing—such as khaki, olive, and beige—is strongly recommended. These colors allow you to blend into the natural environment, which is particularly important during game drives where wildlife is sensitive to movement and contrast. Bright or flashy colors can disrupt animal behavior and reduce your chances of close sightings.

Lightweight fabrics are essential for comfort during the day. However, long-sleeved shirts and trousers should not be overlooked. They protect you from sunburn, insect bites, and scratches, especially during activities like gorilla trekking or nature walks in forested areas.

A warm layer—such as a fleece or jacket—is necessary for early morning drives and evenings. Many first-time visitors are surprised by how cool it can get, particularly in higher-altitude regions.

Footwear is critical. A good pair of hiking boots or strong walking shoes will make a significant difference, especially in muddy or uneven terrain. For lighter activities or relaxation, a pair of comfortable sandals can also be useful.

Health & Protection: Preparing for Uganda’s Natural Environment

Uganda’s beauty comes with exposure to nature in its purest form, and this requires thoughtful preparation. Protecting your health ensures that your safari remains enjoyable from beginning to end.

Insect repellent is essential, particularly in the evenings and near water bodies. Mosquitoes are common in many regions, and protecting yourself reduces the risk of illnesses such as malaria.

Sunscreen is equally important. The equatorial sun can be strong even when the weather feels mild. Without protection, sunburn and fatigue can quickly affect your energy levels.

Carrying personal medication and a basic first aid kit is highly recommended, especially when traveling to remote areas. While Uganda has good medical facilities in cities, access may be limited in national parks.

Simple items like hand sanitizer and wet wipes become extremely valuable during long drives and outdoor activities, where access to clean water may not always be immediate.

Preparing for Uganda’s Unique Safari Activities

One of the most exciting aspects of traveling in Uganda is the variety of experiences available. Unlike many destinations, Uganda offers both classic savannah safaris and intimate forest encounters.

Gorilla trekking in Bwindi Impenetrable Forest is one of the most sought-after experiences in the world. It requires physical effort, as you hike through thick vegetation and uneven terrain. Gloves, long sleeves, and sturdy boots are essential for this activity.

Game drives in parks such as Murchison Falls or Queen Elizabeth National Park involve long hours in a vehicle. Here, comfort is key—light clothing, sun protection, and a camera are must-haves.

Boat cruises along the Nile or Kazinga Channel provide a different perspective of wildlife. While more relaxed, they still require sun protection and hydration.

For those visiting places like Lake Bunyonyi, lighter clothing and relaxation essentials are ideal, allowing you to fully enjoy the peaceful environment.

Essential Gear: Enhancing Every Moment

The right gear does not just add convenience—it transforms your safari experience.

Binoculars allow you to see wildlife clearly from a distance, making them one of the most valuable items you can carry. Many animals stay far from vehicles, and without binoculars, you may miss important details.

A camera or smartphone helps you capture unforgettable moments, from wildlife encounters to stunning landscapes. Extra batteries or a power bank are crucial, especially in lodges where electricity may be limited.

A small backpack is essential for daily use, keeping your items organized and accessible during activities.

Travel Essentials: Documents and Practical Needs

Travel documents are just as important as physical items. A valid passport, visa (if required), and copies of important documents should always be kept secure.

Travel insurance provides peace of mind, covering unexpected situations such as medical emergencies or cancellations.

Carrying some local currency is useful for tips, small purchases, and places where card payments are not accepted.

Final Thoughts: Travel Prepared, Experience Uganda Fully

Uganda is a country of contrasts—wild yet welcoming, adventurous yet peaceful. To fully experience everything it offers, preparation is key.

Packing correctly allows you to move comfortably between forests, savannahs, and lakes without worry. It gives you the freedom to focus on what truly matters—the wildlife, the landscapes, and the people.

Whether you are visiting Uganda for the first time or rediscovering it as a local traveler, being well-prepared ensures that every moment becomes part of an unforgettable journey.

Pack smart, travel confidently, and let Uganda surprise you in ways you never expected.
