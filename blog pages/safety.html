Safety Tips for Travelers in Uganda

Travel smart, stay confident, and enjoy every moment of your journey

By Explore Uganda Travel Desk·Published March 5, 2026

Uganda is widely known as the “Pearl of Africa,” not only for its natural beauty but also for the warmth and friendliness of its people. For many travelers, one of the first questions that comes to mind is: “Is Uganda safe?”

The answer is yes—Uganda is generally safe for travelers, especially those who plan well and follow basic safety guidelines. Like any destination in the world, understanding your environment and taking simple precautions can make all the difference.

This guide is designed to give you clarity, confidence, and practical advice so that you can focus on what truly matters—enjoying your adventure.

General Safety: Awareness is Your Best Tool

Uganda is a welcoming country where visitors are often treated with kindness and curiosity. However, like in any busy city or travel destination, staying aware of your surroundings is important.

In cities such as Kampala, keep your belongings secure, especially in crowded areas like markets or public transport. Avoid displaying expensive items openly, as this may attract unnecessary attention.

Walking during the day is generally safe, but at night it is advisable to use trusted transport options rather than walking long distances. Simple awareness goes a long way in ensuring a smooth experience.

Transportation: Moving Around Safely

Getting around Uganda is part of the adventure, but choosing the right mode of transport is key to your safety and comfort.

For longer journeys, especially between national parks, using a reputable tour operator or private driver is highly recommended. Roads can vary in condition, and local drivers are more familiar with routes and driving practices.

In urban areas, ride-hailing services and registered taxis are safer options compared to informal transport. Motorcycle taxis (boda bodas) are common but should be used with caution, and helmets are strongly recommended.

Health and Well-Being: Staying Protected

Taking care of your health is an important part of traveling in Uganda. The country’s tropical climate means that sun exposure and insects are factors to consider.

Protect yourself from mosquito bites by using insect repellent and sleeping under mosquito nets where provided. This significantly reduces the risk of malaria.

Drink bottled or treated water to avoid stomach issues, and stay hydrated, especially during outdoor activities.

It is also advisable to have travel insurance and carry any personal medication you may need.

Safari Safety: Respecting Wildlife and Nature

One of the highlights of visiting Uganda is the opportunity to experience wildlife up close. While this is exciting, it is important to remember that animals are wild and should be treated with respect.

Always follow the guidance of your safari guide. They are trained to understand animal behavior and ensure your safety.

Keep a safe distance from animals, avoid sudden movements, and never attempt to feed wildlife. These simple rules help protect both you and the animals.

During activities such as gorilla trekking, strict guidelines are in place to ensure a safe and respectful experience. Following these rules is essential.

Money and Personal Belongings

Managing your money wisely contributes to a stress-free trip. Carry only what you need for the day and keep valuables secure.

It is a good idea to use a mix of payment methods, including cash and cards. Keep copies of important documents in case of loss or theft.

Using hotel safes or secure storage for passports and extra cash adds an extra layer of protection.

Respecting Local Culture and Customs

Uganda’s people are one of its greatest strengths. Being respectful of local customs not only ensures your safety but also enriches your experience.

Dress modestly, especially in rural areas, and always ask permission before taking photos of people.

Learning a few local greetings can go a long way in building positive interactions and showing respect.

Final Thoughts: Travel with Confidence

Uganda is a destination that rewards those who explore it with an open mind and a sense of adventure. While it is natural to have concerns about safety, the reality is that most travelers have smooth, enjoyable experiences.

By taking simple precautions—staying aware, respecting local customs, and following guidance—you can travel confidently and fully enjoy everything Uganda has to offer.

Safety is not about fear—it is about preparation. And with the right mindset, your journey through Uganda will be not only safe but truly unforgettable.
